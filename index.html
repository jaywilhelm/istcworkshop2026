Workshop Focus Areas

Technical Themes

🤖

Autonomy at Scale

Deploying autonomous systems across complex multi-domain environments, from edge platforms to enterprise missions.

🧠

Human Interaction & Tasking

Human-machine teaming, trust calibration, and interface design for operator-autonomous system collaboration.

🛡️

Counter-UAS

Intelligent detection, tracking, and neutralization strategies for uncrewed aerial threats in contested airspace.

📡

Precision Navigation

PNT robustness in GPS-denied and contested environments — multi-modal sensing, fusion, and resilient architectures.

⚙️

AI's Impact on Workforce

Responsible AI adoption, transition from academia to government and industry, and the evolving human role in intelligent systems.

🔗

Multi-Domain Intelligent Systems

Cross-cutting IS research spanning air, space, land, sea, and cyber — interoperability, federation, and command architectures.

Next Generation

Student Sessions

Competition

Poster Session

Students present original research across the workshop's intelligent systems themes. Posters are displayed during lunch on Day 1, with dedicated viewing time in the afternoon. This is a chance to engage directly with government, industry, and academic attendees on your work.

Awards

Judging Competition

A panel of ISTC members and guest judges evaluates student posters across criteria including technical merit, relevance to aerospace IS challenges, and presentation quality. Awards are announced at the Student Poster Awards ceremony following lunch on Day 1. No registration fee for students.
